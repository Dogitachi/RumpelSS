Frequently Asked Questions

What are points?

At its core, a point is a digital reward unit, valued for its utility or convertibility into tangible benefits - be it exclusive access, product discounts, or direct monetary value.

Projects deploy point programs strategically, not just to foster loyalty, but to drive product adoption, amplify network effects, and shape user behavior in ways that accelerate product growth.

In Web2, points convert to discount on future purchases. In Web3, points (sometimes) convert to tokens.

Can I sell points that I already have?

Users can sell any supported points earned through the Rumpel Protocol.

Historically rewarded points cannot be sold through Rumpel at the moment.

What are point tokens?

A Rumpel Point Token is a token that represents an offchain point and its future rewards. They map 1:1 or 1:1000 (kilo) with the points earned by users' Rumpel Wallets.

If a project announces an airdrop, point tokens can be redeemed through Rumpel for some reward token.

Do point tokens have a maturity / expiry?

No. While Point Token liquidity can dry up after an airdrop announcement, as users redeem point tokens for their underlying airdrop, any outstanding supply of point tokens can continue to exist as wrappers around some fixed amount of the reward token.

What do I get with a point token?

If a project announces an airdrop, point tokens can be redeemed through Rumpel for some reward token.

By buying point tokens, users can turbo-stack to their preferred exposure without jumping through hoops or exposing their principal to smart contract risk.

Are there fees?

The protocol takes a fee on minting and burning point tokens, and it ensures that point sellers and point buyers are not charged twice.

There will be zero protocol fees at launch and for the foreseeable future

Who's building Rumpel Protocol?

Rumpel is built by Kenton Prescott and a team with experience across MakerDAO, Aave, SpaceX, and EY.

We launched Sense Protocol, a DeFi Protocol from ‘21-’23, are deeply familiar with pointenomics, and hold conviction in its role.
